# NPCIS Framework

A holistic heuristic-evaluation model spanning five dimensions —
**N**avigation, **P**resentation, **C**ontent, **I**nteraction, **S**trategy. Where
Nielsen and Shneiderman are checklists of interaction rules, NPCIS also asks whether the
design serves the **user's and the business's goals**. Cite as "NPCIS — <dimension>".

| Dimension | What it covers | Evaluation lens — look for |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------------|
| **Navigation** | How users move through the product | Logical pathways, clear wayfinding, no dead-ends or unnecessary backtracking; users always know where they are and how to get back. |
| **Presentation** | Visual hierarchy and layout | Scannability, hierarchy that guides the eye, appropriate density, readable typography; the important thing is the prominent thing. |
| **Content** | Language and information | Clear, relevant microcopy, labels, and empty/error states; the user's language, not internal jargon; no missing or misleading information. |
| **Interaction** | Controls, transitions, feedback | Input controls behave predictably; state changes are communicated; transitions and feedback are timely and proportionate. |
| **Strategy** | Alignment to goals | Do the UI elements actively serve the user's goal *and* the stated business goal (conversion, retention, activation)? Flag friction that defeats the goal, and UI that serves neither. |

## Distinctive contribution

NPCIS's unique lens is **Strategy** — the only dimension across the four frameworks that
explicitly asks whether the interface *advances user and business goals*, not just whether
it is usable in the abstract. A flow can pass every Nielsen heuristic and still fail
Strategy (e.g. a friction-free but goal-irrelevant screen). Weight Strategy when the audit
scope includes a conversion or activation path.

The other four dimensions overlap the classic heuristics (Navigation ≈ wayfinding,
Presentation ≈ Nielsen #8 aesthetic/minimalist, Content ≈ Nielsen #2/#9, Interaction ≈
Nielsen #1). When a finding is covered by both, attribute it to its primary framework and
note NPCIS as corroborating — reserve standalone NPCIS citations for Navigation-flow and
Strategy findings the others don't express.
